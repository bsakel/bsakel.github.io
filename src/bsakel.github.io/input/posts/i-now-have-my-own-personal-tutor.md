# I Now Have My Own Personal Tutor

I did not build this idea from scratch.

I found [pi-tutor](https://github.com/denismrvoljak/pi-tutor), loved the tutoring approach, and ported it to OpenCode because it felt genuinely useful for how I learn.

The result is [opencode-tutor](https://github.com/bsakel/opencode-tutor): a tutor mode that prefers hints, questions, and next steps over immediate full answers.

## Why I Wanted It

Most coding assistants are great for shipping fast, but not always great for learning deeply.

When I am learning something new, getting the full answer too early can skip the part where understanding actually happens.

I wanted a mode that helps me think first and solve second.

## What Makes It Useful

- It adapts to my learning style
- It remembers my topics across sessions
- It keeps progress in plain markdown files
- It helps me resume with one concrete next step

That last part matters a lot. I can stop, come back later, and continue without rebuilding context from zero.

## Credit Where It Is Due

The original concept and tutoring methodology come from pi-tutor.

My part was the port: adapting the workflow to OpenCode, aligning it with OpenCode agent/skill discovery, and making it practical for daily use.

## Closing

This is one of those small projects that quietly improved my day-to-day learning.

If AI tools are over-helping you, a tutor-style mode might be the better default.
