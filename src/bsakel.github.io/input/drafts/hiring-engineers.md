# What I Look for When Hiring Engineers

After conducting hundreds of interviews, I have settled on what actually predicts success on a team. Spoiler: it is not algorithm puzzles.

## What Matters

- **Problem decomposition.** Can they break a vague problem into concrete steps?
- **Communication.** Can they explain their thinking clearly? Will they ask clarifying questions?
- **Curiosity.** Do they want to understand why, not just how?
- **Ownership.** Do they take responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks?

## What Matters Less

- Memorized data structures and algorithms
- Years of experience with a specific framework
- Whether they can code on a whiteboard under pressure

## My Interview Structure

1. A short technical screen focused on real-world problems
2. A system design discussion (for senior roles)
3. A conversation about past projects and how they handled challenges
4. A team-fit chat with future colleagues

Hire for trajectory, not just current skill level.
