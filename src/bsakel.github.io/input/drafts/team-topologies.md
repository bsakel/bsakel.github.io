# Applying Team Topologies in Practice

Team Topologies by Skelton and Pais changed how I think about organizing engineering teams. The core idea is simple: team structure should follow the architecture you want, not the other way around.

## The Four Team Types

1. **Stream-aligned** teams own a slice of the business domain end-to-end.
2. **Platform** teams provide self-service capabilities to reduce cognitive load.
3. **Enabling** teams help stream-aligned teams adopt new skills or technologies.
4. **Complicated subsystem** teams own areas requiring deep specialist knowledge.

## What I Learned

Most organizations have too many component teams and not enough stream-aligned teams. When I restructured my teams along domain boundaries instead of technical layers, deployment frequency went up and cross-team coordination went down.

The book is worth reading. The real value is in the interaction modes between teams, not just the labels.
