# Making One-on-Ones Actually Useful

One-on-ones are the most important recurring meeting a manager has. But too many managers treat them as status updates. That is a waste of everyone's time.

## What One-on-Ones Are For

- Building trust
- Giving and receiving feedback
- Career development
- Removing blockers the person cannot remove themselves

## What They Are Not For

- Status updates (use standup or async updates)
- Project planning (use planning meetings)
- Performance reviews (those happen separately)

## My Format

I let the direct report set the agenda. If they have nothing, I come prepared with questions:

- What is the most frustrating part of your work right now?
- Is there anything you would change about how the team works?
- What do you want to be doing in a year that you are not doing now?

## Consistency Matters

Weekly, 30 minutes, rarely cancelled. The rhythm builds trust. If you keep cancelling one-on-ones, you are telling your team they are not a priority.
